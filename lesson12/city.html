Rigby, Idaho

Rigby's History

The town of Rigby was officially established in 1903. Many settlers came to Rigby before it was officially established. A majority of the settlers who came to Rigby belonged to the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Rigby got its name from one of the pioneers settlers named William F. Rigby. Something that many people may not be aware of is that Rigby is the birthplace of television. For more information about the history of Rigby, Idaho, please visit this link.

Attractions

One of Rigby's most interesting tourist attractions is the Farnsworth TV and Pioneer Museum. Tourists who visit Rigby will have the opportunity to learn about how the pioneers settled in Rigby and how the television was created. This museum is located at 118 W 1st S, Rigby, ID 83442. For more information about the Farnsworth TV and Pioneer Museum, please vist this link.
Another one of Rigby's biggest attractions is the Jefferson County Lake. Many people go there in the summer time to enjoy sitting in the sun with friends and family. A fireworks celebration is held around the 4th of July to celebrate Independence Day.
